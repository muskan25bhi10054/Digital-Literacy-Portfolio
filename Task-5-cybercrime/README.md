# Personal Reflection #

Researching UPI fraud was eye-opening because I realized how easily "social engineering" can bypass even the best security features. What surprised me most was that attackers don't need to "hack" a system; they simply need to exploit human psychology and the fast-paced nature of digital life.

One habit I will personally change is my use of QR codes. I used to scan them mindlessly at shops or online, but I now realize that a malicious QR code can lead to phishing sites or unauthorized payment requests. As a Health Informatics student, I also recognize that protecting my financial identity is the first step toward protecting the sensitive medical data I will handle in my future career. I am now committed to using a dedicated password manager to ensure I never reuse passwords across different platforms.
