# Task 1: Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic #
Tool Used:
For this project, I utilized Adobe Express to design my visual resource. I chose this specific platform because it offers a sophisticated balance of professional templates and customizable elements. As a Student Digital Ambassador, it was important to use a tool that allowed me to incorporate high-quality icons and a clear visual hierarchy, ensuring the final product was both educational and aesthetically pleasing for my peers.

Design Overview:
My infographic is strategically divided into three core sections to address the essential components of digital literacy. First, I provided a comprehensive definition of Digital Literacy, moving beyond basic computer skills to include critical thinking and effective communication. Second, I highlighted Useful Digital Tools, such as Notion for organization and Canva for creativity, which are vital for modern academic success. Finally, I focused on Safe Internet Practices, providing actionable advice on two-factor authentication and data privacy to help students protect their digital identities.

Reflection:
Through this process, I realized that being digitally literate is not just a technical requirement but a fundamental part of being a responsible digital citizen. The most challenging aspect was synthesizing complex security information into concise, impactful bullet points that fit the design without cluttering the page. This task improved my ability to communicate technical information visually and reinforced the importance of maintaining a professional online presence.

# Task 3: Coding and Collaboration Platforms #
Platforms Used:
For this task, I explored HackerRank for coding practice and Google Workspace (Forms and Sheets) for digital collaboration. These tools are essential for a Computer Science student to build technical logic and manage data effectively.

Project Overview:
In Part A, I used HackerRank to solve the "Python Loops" challenge. This exercise required me to write a script that calculates and prints the square of non-negative integers. Successfully passing the test cases improved my understanding of Python syntax and algorithmic thinking. In Part B, I developed a "Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz" using Google Forms. This included a variety of question types like multiple-choice and short-answer to engage batchmates. I then linked this form to a Google Sheet to demonstrate how to automate data collection and organize responses in real-time.

Academic Impact:
Practicing on HackerRank prepares me for technical interviews and competitive programming, which are vital for my specialization in Health Informatics. Meanwhile, mastering Google Workspace tools allows me to collaborate on research projects and create structured surveys for health data collection. These platforms empower me to be a more effective Digital Ambassador by providing the tools to teach and lead others in a digital-first academic environment.
